# Blueprint: Formalizing Hecke Operators ($T_p$) for Modular Forms in Lean 4

## 1. Overview
This blueprint outlines the required definitions, theorems, and structural steps to formalize the Hecke operators ($T_p$) for modular forms in Lean 4's `Mathlib`. The implementation must integrate smoothly with the existing `Mathlib.NumberTheory.ModularForms` API, utilizing the `SlashAction` and preserving the properties of `ModularForm ╬ô k`.

## 2. Compatibility with `Mathlib.NumberTheory.ModularForms`
The existing `Mathlib.NumberTheory.ModularForms.Basic` API defines `ModularForm ╬ô k` as holomorphic functions on the upper half-plane ($\mathbb{H}$) that are invariant under the weight $k$ slash action of a congruence subgroup $\Gamma \le \text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ and are holomorphic at the cusps.

### 2.1 Slash Action
- Mathlib defines the slash action for $GL_2^+(\mathbb{R})$ on functions $\mathbb{H} \to \mathbb{C}$. 
- The Hecke operator $T_p$ relies on the slash action of matrices with determinant $p$.
- **Action Definition**: For a modular form $`f \in M_k(\Gamma)`$ and prime $p$, the operator $`T_p`$ is defined as a sum over left coset representatives $`\gamma_j`$:

$$
(T_p f)(z) = p^{k/2 - 1} \sum_j (f |_k \gamma_j)(z)
$$
  *(Note: Normalization conventions vary; some use $p^{k-1}$. The API should specify the chosen normalizer, commonly $p^{k-1}$ for integer coefficients.)*

## 3. Double Coset Decomposition
The foundation of the Hecke algebra is the double coset decomposition. We must define the action abstractly and explicitly for $`\Gamma = \text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})`$ or $`\Gamma_0(N)`$.

### 3.1. Abstract Double Cosets
- **Definition**: Given subgroups $H, K \le G$ and $g \in G$, the double coset $H g K$ is the set $\{ h g k \mid h \in H, k \in K \}$.
- **Finiteness Hypothesis**: We need a typeclass or a lemma proving that for our chosen $\Gamma$ and $`\alpha_p = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & p \end{pmatrix}`$, the double coset $`\Gamma \alpha_p \Gamma`$ decomposes into a **finite** disjoint union of left cosets $`\bigcup_{j \in J} \Gamma \gamma_j`$.
- **Mathlib Representation**: Utilize or extend `MulAction` and orbit structures to describe `╬ô \ (╬ô * ╬▒ * ╬ô)`.

### 3.2. Explicit Representatives for $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$
For $`\Gamma = \text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})`$ and a prime $p$, the explicit left coset representatives $`R_p`$ are:

$$
R_p = \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & j \\ 0 & p \end{pmatrix} \;\middle|\; 0 \le j \lt p \right\} \cup \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} p & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \right\}
$$

**Lean Tasks:**
- Define `heckeCosetReps (p : Ôäò) : Finset (Matrix (Fin 2) (Fin 2) Ôäñ)`.
- Prove `lemma hecke_double_coset_decomp (p : Ôäò) [Fact p.Prime]`: Show that $`\Gamma \alpha_p \Gamma = \bigsqcup_{\gamma \in R_p} \Gamma \gamma`$.

## 4. Action on Lattices (The Geometric Perspective)
Hecke operators naturally act on the free abelian group generated by lattices in $\mathbb{C}$. This provides an essential conceptual tool for proving commutativity ($`T_p T_q = T_q T_p`$).

### 4.1. Lattice Equivalences
- A lattice $\Lambda \subset \mathbb{C}$ corresponds to a left coset of $`\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})`$ in $`GL_2^+(\mathbb{R})`$.
- The operator $`T_p`$ on lattices maps $`\Lambda \mapsto \sum_{[\Lambda : \Lambda'] = p} \Lambda'`$.
- **Lean Tasks:**
  - Define `Sublattice.index : Submodule Ôäñ Ôäé ÔåÆ Ôäò`.
  - Prove the bijection between `heckeCosetReps p` and index-$p$ sublattices of the standard lattice $\langle 1, \tau \rangle$.

## 5. Defining the Operator and Proving Modularity
To upgrade the operator to `ModularForm ╬ô k ÔåÆÔéù[Ôäé] ModularForm ╬ô k`, we must verify three properties:

### 5.1. Slash Invariance
- Summing over a complete set of left cosets ensures that right multiplication by any $\gamma \in \Gamma$ merely permutes the cosets.
- **Goal**: `lemma hecke_slash_invariant (f : SlashInvariantForm ╬ô k) : (heckeOperator p k f) |_k ╬│ = (heckeOperator p k f)`

### 5.2. Holomorphicity on $\mathbb{H}$
- Since $f$ is holomorphic and each $\gamma \in R_p$ acts as a holomorphic transformation, a finite sum of holomorphic functions is holomorphic.
- **Goal**: `lemma hecke_holomorphic (f : ModularForm ╬ô k) : HolomorphicOn (heckeOperator p k f) Ôäì`

### 5.3. Holomorphicity at Cusps (Fourier Expansion)
- The action on the Fourier expansion confirms behavior at infinity.
- If $`f(z) = \sum a_n q^n`$, then $`(T_p f)(z) = \sum b_n q^n`$ where $`b_n = a_{np} + p^{k-1} a_{n/p}`$.
- **Goal**: `lemma hecke_fourier_coeff (f : ModularForm ╬ô k) (n : Ôäò) : hecke_coeff p f n = f.coeff (n * p) + p^(k-1) * f.coeff (n / p)` (with $`a_{n/p} = 0`$ if $p \nmid n$).
- Since $`b_n = 0`$ for $n \lt 0$, $`T_p f`$ is holomorphic at $\infty$. If $f$ is a cusp form, $`b_0 = 0`$, making it stable on `CuspForm ╬ô k`.

## 7. Future Generalization
Once $`T_p`$ is formalized for $`\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})`$, the abstract structure should be extensible to:
- General congruence subgroups $`\Gamma_0(N)`$ and $`\Gamma_1(N)`$ (handling $p \mid N$ cases via $`U_p`$ operators).
- The full Hecke Algebra $\mathbb{T}$ generated by $\{ T_p \}$.
- The spectral theorem: the existence of Hecke eigenforms (a basis of simultaneous eigenvectors for all $T_p$).
