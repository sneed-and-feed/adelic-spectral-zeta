During training, token embeddings are projected into branching paths on a Bruhat-Tits tree. The coordinate indices mapping branch matches are sorted and sent as indices to the Triton hardware kernel, avoiding the materialization of the full O(N²) attention scores in HBM.

To sparsify pre-trained dense LLMs without destroying world knowledge, we deploy a Continuous Logit Homotopy. Router projection weights are initialized negative to match the pre-trained dense manifold exactly, before annealing and Gumbel-temperature curves gradually polarize attention heads into discrete, hardware-efficient block-sparse subtrees.

Theoretical Mappings

1. From p-adic Distance to Differentiable Masks

In the adèlic library, the $p$-adic distance matrix is computed using prime valuations ($v_p$). The distance decreases as the power of the prime dividing the coordinate differences increases:

d_p(x, y) = p^{-v_p(x - y)}

In the transformer router, this is replaced by the differentiable expectation of tree distance based on path matching probabilities across levels:

expected_dist = levels - sum(cumprod(agreement_probs))

2. Adèlic Product Formula & Multi-Prime Routing

The adèlic product formula connects all prime places to reconstruct global algebraic spaces:

A_Q = R x \prod_p Q_p

The V2 research module implements this by grouping the attention heads and assigning co-prime arities (e.g. $p=2$ and $p=3$ concurrently). The intersection of their local tree structures covers the sequence, replicating the global properties of adèlic numbers.
